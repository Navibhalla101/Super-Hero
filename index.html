SEC / 01 The World Before Super-Heroes

I've always wondered what a world
looks like when no one is coming
to save us.

No capes. No symbols. No one above us.

So we turned to each other. Built cities that reflected that choice — repeating, identical, stable. No one stands apart. No one stands above.

We rejected the idea of the exceptional.

But I can't help asking — if no one ever believed in a hero... what would it take to become one?

SEC / 02 What Even Is a Super-Hero?

Throughout the last ninety years, we've reveled in science fiction and ancient astronauts portraying unfathomable gods among us.

What if they were just people trying to do the right thing? I think that's the part we keep skipping. We keep drawing the cape first and the person second, when the person is the whole point.

A super-hero is a proposal, not a discovery. Somebody, somewhere, sat down and said here is what a good person could be if we took off the ceiling. And then enough of us believed it that the idea became real enough to imitate.

SEC / 05 Iconography — what the costumes mean

The bright warm colors, the wacky gadgets, and accentuated armor are not flashiness.

There are deeper meanings within why a hero puts on a costume in the first place. It is not for them to be noticed — it is for them to spread a message of positivity, hope, independence, protection, or purely represent the values of a community, a region, an object.

SEC / 07 My Impetus
Placeholder — self portrait goes here.

I learned how to write kind characters because I grew up with kind parents.

That's really the simplest way to say it. Before I ever sat down and tried to come up with larger-than-life people, I already knew what made someone worth admiring. It wasn't power. It wasn't fearlessness. It wasn't even brilliance.

It was kindness. My mom and dad gave me that idea early, not by explaining it like a lesson, but by living it every day in ways that were easy to miss if you weren't paying attention.

So when I started writing, and there was no real tradition for the kinds of heroic figures I wanted to create, I didn't invent them out of nowhere. I built them from what I already knew. I gave them impossible abilities, yes — but the part that actually mattered came from my parents.

Someone with that much power shouldn't be defined by strength alone. They have to be strong, but to be gentle.

Every kind character I ever wrote was me trying to put my mother and father into fiction, then making them larger so the whole world could see what I had already seen.

If we understood that — imagine how high we can get.
